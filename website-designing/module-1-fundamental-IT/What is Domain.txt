What Is Domain ?

Domain Definition:
A domain is a distinct area of knowledge, activity, or control. In computing, a domain often refers to a group of computers and resources on a network that are administered as a unit with common rules and procedures.

Types of Domains:

1. Internet Domain:
    - Definition: A unique name that identifies a website on the internet.
    - Example: google.com

2. Mathematical Domain:
    - Definition: The set of all possible input values (independent variable) for a function.
    - Example: For f(x) = x^2, the domain is all real numbers.

3. Network Domain:
    - Definition: A collection of computers and devices on a network that are managed as a single entity.

4. Business Domain:
    - Definition: A specific area of business or industry.
    - Example: Healthcare domain, Finance domain.
