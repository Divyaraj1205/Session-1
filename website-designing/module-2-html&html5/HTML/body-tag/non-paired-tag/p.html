Shri Krishna Janmashtami is celebrated with great devotion and enthusiasm across India. Devotees observe fasts, sing bhajans, and decorate temples with flowers and lights to welcome the birth of Lord Krishna at midnight.

One of the highlights of Janmashtami is the Dahi Handi celebration, where young devotees form human pyramids to break a pot of curd, symbolizing Krishna’s love for butter and curd.

Many people also perform Raas Garba and devotional dances to honor Krishna’s playful childhood. The air is filled with chants of “Hare Krishna, Hare Rama,” spreading positivity and divine energy.
