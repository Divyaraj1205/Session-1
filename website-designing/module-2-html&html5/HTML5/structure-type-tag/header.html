Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival that celebrates the birth of Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles and the god of wisdom and prosperity.

The festival usually lasts for 10 days, starting on the fourth day of the Hindu month Bhadrapada, which generally falls in August or September.

During Ganesh Chaturthi, beautifully crafted idols of Lord Ganesha are installed in homes and public pandals, decorated with flowers and lights.

Devotees offer sweets, fruits, and the famous 'modak' as prasad, which is believed to be Lord Ganesha’s favorite sweet.

The chanting of 'Ganpati Bappa Morya' fills the air, symbolizing devotion and joy throughout the celebration.

Cultural activities, music, and dance performances are organized in communities to mark the festive spirit of Ganesh Chaturthi.

On the final day, the idols are taken in grand processions for immersion in rivers, lakes, or the sea, symbolizing Ganesha’s return to Mount Kailash.

The immersion, known as 'Visarjan,' is accompanied by prayers, singing, and dancing, spreading positivity and unity.

Ganesh Chaturthi is not just a religious event but also a festival that unites families, friends, and communities in celebration.
